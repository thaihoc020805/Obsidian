The line charts illustrates how three different sectors emitted air pollutants in the UK, along with total emissions, between 1990 and 2005.

Overall, The total amount of air pollutants followed a downward trend, which was mirrored by those of industry and households, whereas transport emissions remained fairly stable. Additionally, the industrial sector consistently accounted for the highest emissions while households released the lowest amount for the majority of the period.

Industry was the main source of emissions, yet it recorded a threefold reduction over the period. At the beginning, this sector produced 6 million tonnes of air pollutants, although by 1996, the figure halved. From this point, emissions continued to fall, albeit at a slower rate, ending the period at precisely 2 million tonnes.

By contrast, the remaining two sources were relatively minor contributors to overall emissions throughout the period. Air pollutants from transport, which generally the larger source of the two, followed a broadly stable pattern, fluctuating minimally around 1 million tonnes throughout the period. As for households, an identical amount of pollutants was initally emitted from this source as was produced by transport. However, they subsequently fell by nearly 0.4 million tonnes in 1996. Despite a briefly recovery to approximately 0.7 million tonnes over the following three year, the figure receded to about 0.2 million tonnes by the end.

The sharp fall in industry combined with minimal changes elsewhere largely dictated  the pattern of the overall air pollution levels.As a result, the total amount of air pollutants plunged from nearly 8 million tonnes to roughly 4.5 million tonnes over the first six year, followed by a more gradual decline to approximately 3.5 million tonnes by 2005
